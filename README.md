# TextSmith2

> A focused writing tool for novelists who want to write and not fiddle with software.

TextSmith2 is a Scrivener-inspired writing environment built around a single idea: **get out of the writer's way**. No subscription. No cloud sync required. No feature sprawl. Just your manuscript and a clean place to write.

---

## Why TextSmith2?

Scrivener is powerful. It's also a lot. If you've ever spent forty-five minutes organizing corkboards instead of writing your chapter, you know the problem.

TextSmith2 takes the parts that actually matter to novelists (a structured project tree, a clean editor, and the ability to see your whole manuscript at a glance) and leaves out everything else.

---

## Features

### 🌲 Flexible Project Tree
Your novel is organized as a tree structure in the sidebar. Each node carries **tags** that you define, things like `chapter`, `scene`, `part`, `prologue`, or whatever fits your story's structure. You tell TextSmith2 what those tags mean, and it uses them to make sense of your manuscript. No rigid templates. Your story, your structure.

### ✍️ Distraction-Free Writing
The editor is clean by default. No toolbars fighting for attention. No notifications. No clutter. The words are the whole point, and the interface knows it.

### 🖥️ Full-Screen Mode
One keypress takes you to full-screen writing mode. Everything disappears except your current scene and the cursor. For writers who need to go deep, this is where it happens.

### ⌨️ Typewriter Sounds *(optional)*
Full-screen mode includes optional typewriter sound effects as you type. It's a small thing that a lot of writers find surprisingly effective for getting into flow. Toggle it on or off, I'm not judging either way.

## Contributing

TextSmith2 is open source and contributions are welcome. If you're a novelist who also writes code, you probably have strong opinions about what a writing tool should do. Bring them.

- **Bug reports**: Open an issue with your OS and a description of what happened.
- **Feature requests**: Open an issue. Keep in mind the project's core philosophy: features that reduce focus won't be a good fit, but features that serve the writing will get a fair look.
- **macOS testing**: Especially welcome. See platform support above.
- **Pull requests**: Please open an issue first to discuss significant changes.

---

## Philosophy

TextSmith2 isn't trying to be everything. It's trying to be exactly enough: a tool that respects that the hard part of writing a novel is the writing, and that software's job is to make that easier, not more complicated.

If you need a full production/publishing pipeline, Scrivener is probably the right tool. If you want to sit down, open your manuscript, and write then TextSmith2 is built for you.
